Bilt Credit Card for Credit Building: Does It Help?
  • Home
  • Blog
  • Blog Details

In today’s financial landscape, building and maintaining a strong credit score is more crucial than ever. With rising interest rates, stricter lending standards, and the increasing cost of living, having good credit can mean the difference between securing a mortgage at a reasonable rate or being locked out of homeownership altogether. One financial product that has gained attention in recent years is the Bilt Credit Card, marketed as a tool for renters to build credit while earning rewards on rent payments. But does it actually help with credit building? Let’s dive in.

Understanding the Bilt Credit Card

The Bilt Credit Card is unique because it allows users to pay rent without incurring transaction fees—a rarity in the credit card world. Typically, landlords and property management companies charge a 2.5% to 3% fee for credit card rent payments, but Bilt bypasses this by processing rent payments through ACH transfers.

Key Features of the Bilt Card

  • No Annual Fee – Unlike many rewards cards, Bilt doesn’t charge an annual fee.
  • Rent Payments Without Fees – Users can earn points on rent without extra costs.
  • Rewards on Everyday Spending – The card offers points on dining, travel, and other purchases.
  • Credit Reporting – Bilt reports to all three major credit bureaus (Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion).

How Credit Building Works

Before assessing whether the Bilt Card helps with credit building, it’s essential to understand how credit scores are calculated. The FICO score, the most widely used credit scoring model, considers:

  1. Payment History (35%) – Whether you pay bills on time.
  2. Credit Utilization (30%) – The percentage of available credit you’re using.
  3. Length of Credit History (15%) – How long your accounts have been open.
  4. Credit Mix (10%) – The variety of credit types (loans, credit cards, etc.).
  5. New Credit (10%) – Recent credit inquiries and new accounts.

The Role of a Credit Card in Credit Building

Credit cards can be powerful tools for building credit because they influence multiple factors:

  • Payment History – Consistent on-time payments improve your score.
  • Credit Utilization – Keeping balances low (ideally under 30%) helps.
  • Credit Mix & Age – A well-managed card adds diversity and longevity to your credit profile.

Does the Bilt Card Actually Help Build Credit?

Now, the big question: Does the Bilt Credit Card effectively help users build credit? The short answer is yes, but with some caveats.

Pros of Using Bilt for Credit Building

  1. Reports to All Three Bureaus

    • Many credit cards report to only one or two bureaus, but Bilt reports to all three, ensuring your positive payment history is fully reflected.

  2. No Fee Rent Payments = More On-Time Payments

    • Since rent is often the largest monthly expense, paying it on time via Bilt can significantly boost payment history.

  3. Potential for Low Utilization

    • If you use the card responsibly (e.g., paying off balances in full), your credit utilization stays low, which is good for your score.

  4. Helps Establish Credit History

    • For young adults or those new to credit, Bilt can serve as an entry point to building a credit profile.

Potential Downsides

  1. Requires Discipline

    • Like any credit card, misuse (late payments, high balances) can hurt your score.

  2. Limited Spending Rewards

    • While rent payments earn points, other spending categories may not be as rewarding as premium travel cards.

  3. Not a Standalone Solution

    • Credit building works best with a mix of credit types (e.g., installment loans, multiple credit cards).

Real-World Impact: Case Studies & Data

Success Stories

Many users, particularly renters in expensive cities like New York and San Francisco, have reported significant credit score improvements after using Bilt responsibly for 6-12 months. For example:

  • User A: Started with a 620 FICO score, used Bilt for rent and paid balances in full. After 10 months, their score rose to 720.
  • User B: Had a thin credit file (only a student loan). After adding Bilt and using it for daily expenses (paid in full each month), their score jumped from 580 to 670 in a year.

Industry Insights

According to a 2023 report by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB), consumers who use credit cards for recurring bills (like rent) and pay them off consistently see faster credit score growth than those who rely solely on debit or cash.

How to Maximize Credit Building with Bilt

If you’re considering the Bilt Card to boost your credit, follow these best practices:

1. Always Pay On Time

  • Set up autopay to avoid missed payments.

2. Keep Utilization Below 30%

  • Even better, aim for under 10% for optimal scoring.

3. Use It for Other Purchases

  • Adding small, regular purchases (like groceries) and paying them off helps demonstrate responsible usage.

4. Monitor Your Credit Report

  • Check for errors and ensure Bilt’s payments are being reported correctly.

Final Thoughts

The Bilt Credit Card is a legitimate tool for credit building, especially for renters who want to turn their largest expense into a credit-boosting opportunity. However, like any financial product, its effectiveness depends on how you use it. Responsible usage—timely payments, low balances, and long-term consistency—will yield the best results.

For those struggling with thin or poor credit, Bilt offers a unique advantage by allowing rent payments to contribute to credit history. But remember, no single product is a magic bullet. Pairing Bilt with other credit-building strategies (like secured cards or credit-builder loans) can accelerate your progress toward a strong financial future.
