Best Credit Cards for Bad Credit in 2024 (Approval Chances)
  • Home
  • Blog
  • Blog Details

Rebuilding credit can feel like an uphill battle, especially in today’s unpredictable economy. With rising inflation, fluctuating interest rates, and tighter lending standards, securing a credit card with bad credit is tougher than ever. But don’t lose hope—several financial institutions now offer cards designed specifically for those looking to rebuild their credit scores.

In this guide, we’ll break down the best credit cards for bad credit in 2024, focusing on approval chances, fees, rewards, and how they can help you improve your financial standing.

Why Your Credit Score Matters More Than Ever

The Global Credit Crunch

Post-pandemic economic recovery has been uneven, and lenders have become more cautious. Banks are scrutinizing credit histories more closely, making it harder for those with low scores to qualify for traditional cards.

Inflation & Rising Interest Rates

With the Federal Reserve hiking interest rates to combat inflation, borrowing costs have surged. If you have bad credit, you’ll likely face higher APRs—making it crucial to choose a card with manageable terms.

The Rise of Alternative Credit Scoring

Some issuers now consider factors like rent and utility payments when evaluating applications. This shift benefits those with thin credit files but doesn’t eliminate the need for responsible credit card use.

Top Credit Cards for Bad Credit in 2024

1. Discover it® Secured Credit Card

Approval Chance: High (Requires refundable security deposit)
Best For: Cashback rewards + credit building

  • Key Features:

    • Earn 2% cash back at gas stations & restaurants (up to $1,000 per quarter)
    • $0 annual fee
    • Reports to all three major credit bureaus

  • Why It Stands Out:
    Unlike most secured cards, this one offers rewards—a rare perk for bad-credit applicants. After 8 months, Discover may review your account for an upgrade to an unsecured card.

2. Capital One Platinum Secured Credit Card

Approval Chance: Moderate to High (Flexible deposit options)
Best For: Low upfront costs

  • Key Features:

    • Deposit as low as $49 for a $200 credit line (subject to eligibility)
    • No annual fee
    • Potential credit limit increase after 6 months

  • Why It Stands Out:
    Capital One is known for being lenient with applicants who have poor credit. The low minimum deposit makes it accessible.

3. OpenSky® Secured Visa® Credit Card

Approval Chance: Very High (No credit check required)
Best For: Those with recent bankruptcies or charge-offs

  • Key Features:

    • No credit check—approval based on deposit ($200–$3,000)
    • $35 annual fee
    • Helps rebuild credit with bureau reporting

  • Why It Stands Out:
    If your credit is severely damaged, this card offers a near-guaranteed approval since it doesn’t pull your credit report.

4. Chime Credit Builder Secured Visa®

Approval Chance: High (Must have a Chime checking account)
Best For: Fee-free credit building

  • Key Features:

    • No interest, no annual fee
    • Uses your own money as collateral (like a debit card)
    • No hard credit pull

  • Why It Stands Out:
    This card works more like a debit card but reports as a credit account, making it ideal for cautious spenders.

How to Improve Approval Odds

Check Your Credit Report First

Errors on your report can drag down your score. Use AnnualCreditReport.com to review your file for inaccuracies before applying.

Start with a Secured Card

Since these require a deposit, they’re easier to get. Over time, responsible use can help you qualify for unsecured cards.

Avoid Multiple Applications

Each application triggers a hard inquiry, which can further lower your score. Space out applications by at least 6 months.

Consider a Co-Signer or Authorized User Status

If possible, ask a trusted friend or family member with good credit to add you as an authorized user on their account.

The Future of Credit Cards for Bad Credit

AI-Driven Underwriting

Some fintech companies now use machine learning to assess risk beyond traditional FICO scores, which could expand access to credit.

"Buy Now, Pay Later" (BNPL) Reporting

Services like Affirm and Klarna are starting to report payments to credit bureaus, offering another way to build credit without a card.

Regulatory Changes

The CFPB is pushing for more transparency in credit scoring, which may lead to fairer approval processes for subprime borrowers.

Rebuilding credit won’t happen overnight, but the right card can set you on the path to financial recovery. Choose wisely, spend responsibly, and monitor your progress—your future self will thank you.
