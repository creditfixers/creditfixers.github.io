Navy Federal Cash Rewards vs. Other Military Credit Cards
  • Home
  • Blog
  • Blog Details

When it comes to military credit cards, service members, veterans, and their families have unique financial needs. Whether it’s maximizing cash back, earning travel rewards, or enjoying low-interest rates, choosing the right card can make a big difference. The Navy Federal Cash Rewards Credit Card is a popular choice, but how does it stack up against other military-focused credit cards? Let’s dive into the details.

Why Military Credit Cards Are Different

Military credit cards often come with perks tailored to the lifestyle of service members. These may include:
- No foreign transaction fees (great for deployments)
- Lower APRs compared to civilian cards
- Exclusive rewards for military-related spending
- Flexible payment options during deployments

Given today’s economic climate—rising inflation, unpredictable interest rates, and global instability—having a card that aligns with military life is more important than ever.

Navy Federal Cash Rewards: The Breakdown

Key Features

  • 1.5% cash back on all purchases
  • No annual fee
  • 0% intro APR for 12 months on balance transfers (then 18.00%–18.00% variable)
  • No foreign transaction fees

Pros

✔ Simple rewards structure – No complicated categories.
✔ Military-friendly – Navy Federal is a credit union exclusively for service members and their families.
✔ Strong customer service – Known for understanding military-specific financial challenges.

Cons

✖ Lower cash back rate – Some competitors offer 2% or higher.
✖ Limited redemption options – Cash back can only be redeemed as a statement credit or deposit.

Top Competitors to Navy Federal Cash Rewards

1. USAA Cashback Rewards Plus American Express®

  • 2.5% cash back on all purchases (if you maintain a USAA checking account and direct deposit).
  • No annual fee.
  • No foreign transaction fees.

Verdict: If you’re already banking with USAA, this card is a no-brainer for higher rewards.

2. PenFed Power Cash Rewards Visa®

  • 2% cash back on all purchases.
  • No annual fee.
  • Must be a PenFed member (but membership is open to all military personnel).

Verdict: A strong alternative if you want a straightforward 2% cash back card.

3. Chase Sapphire Preferred® (for Military Members)

  • 3X points on dining, online groceries, and streaming.
  • 5X points on travel booked through Chase.
  • Annual fee waived for active-duty military under the SCRA.

Verdict: Best for those who prefer travel rewards over cash back.

Which Card Should You Choose?

If You Want Simple Cash Back

  • Navy Federal Cash Rewards (1.5%) is decent, but PenFed Power Cash Rewards (2%) is better.

If You Want Maximum Rewards

  • USAA Cashback Rewards Plus (2.5%) wins—if you qualify.

If You Want Travel Perks

  • Chase Sapphire Preferred® is the top pick, especially with its waived annual fee for military.

The Impact of Global Events on Military Finances

With ongoing conflicts in Ukraine and the Middle East, many service members are facing unexpected deployments. A credit card with no foreign transaction fees and flexible payment options is crucial. Additionally, rising interest rates mean that low APR cards (like Navy Federal’s intro 0% offer) can help manage debt better.

Final Thoughts

While the Navy Federal Cash Rewards card is a solid choice, it’s not always the best. Depending on your spending habits and financial goals, another military credit card might serve you better. Always compare fees, rewards, and benefits before applying.

(Note: Always check the latest terms, as credit card offers can change.)
